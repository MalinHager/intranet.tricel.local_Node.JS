Natural Resources

Tricell resolves challenges in the sustainable and profitable use of renewable natural resources in four phenomenon-based research programmes that steer research in accordance with our strategy.
 
The programmes build and develop cooperation with different stakeholders using such means as round table discussions. Furthermore, the programmes open up new connections inside Tricell by bringing specialists and expertise together and by supporting multidisciplinary researcher networks.

Natural Resources

Adaptive and Resilient Bioeconomy The research programmes have set their sights firmly on the future. They build Tricell’s effectiveness internationally and aim to have a societal impact.
 
Our research programmes are: - Profitable and responsible primary production - Circular bioeconomy - Climate smart carbon cycle - Adaptive and resilient bioeconomy

Water, Air, and Soil
 
- Water is one of the most important natural resources flowing from forests.
 
- Soil provides nutrients, water, oxygen and heat to natural land areas.
 
- Air is a third critical resource for humans, plants, animals and all other organisms within a natural area.
